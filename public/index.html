Mahrokh Ghoddousi Boroujeni

I am a PhD student at EPFL in Lausanne, Switzerland, supervised by Prof. Giancarlo Ferrari Trecate and co-supervised by Prof. Andreas Krause . My research focuses on employing advanced machine learning techniques to develop complex controllers for intricate tasks.

Learning models and controllers from data has become ubiquitous, yet it often involves limited and noisy datasets. I study the impact of such factors on the performance and reliability of learned models and controllers. Specifically, I aim to develop models and controllers that generalize well and maintain stability beyond their training data. I also explore the uncertainty in the learned model due to noise using probabilistic modeling. Finally, I am interested in leveraging multiple related datasets through meta-learning and federated learning approaches to enhance performance and robustness.
